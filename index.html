All the Knowledge of the World

History is divided into two sections: before and after Christ. Everything before was a search for Him, everything after an adjustment to Him.

The theandric reality is liturgical and the people who assemble that reality are the Church, and the culmination of life in this reality is at Divine Liturgy.

Life in Christ is a paradox.

You are and you aren't.

What you do matters and doesn't matter.

You matter and you don't matter.

There is a life after death and there is no life after death.

But there is definitely life according to this theandric reality and death and evil outside of it.

Only the Orthodox Church is the institutional repository of all knowledge and truth.

All of these designations are not covering a spectrum or general in any sense. They have a very specific meaning that cannot be learned — it, like all knowledge, needs to be formed, and it is only formed in a person/as a person.

Not all formed people are liturgical.

There are many more people that are not formed at all, but are like animals.

These are the people who mimic the sounds other people make, no different from AI, and there indeed is no difference between them other than that the people are trapped in this state. They have forgotten to speak. For them language is for complaining and judging. No longer people. The living dead.

They do not use language for personal formation. The only thing language can be used for.

You can use language to form into an accuser of the world that is the Devil, but you are forming into a phantom: bits and pieces of reality reordered temporarily by the free will granted to some creatures in creation, of which people are the lowest and the most exalted.

Idol worship is a relationship of reward and punishment.

All of creation belongs to God. You belong to who you belong.

Nothing else is knowledge.
